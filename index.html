TakeMeHome

Government Research Product
Project

Our Client was participating in a government research project on management of patient escapes from healthcare facilities. The client asked us to create a prototype that can be used at mental health facilities for testing.

Approach

We designed, created, and delivered a prototype system for the project. The system used low cost hardware to detect patient escape events and enable location tracking. The backend software provided real time notifications on a web client, as well as text (SMS) messages.

Our choice of hardware solutions provided many performance and usability benefits over current solutions using RFID and GPS. Software architecture was designed to significantly lower latency compared to current solutions, as well as handle heavy load on minimal server costs.

Technologies

The project featured solutions such as:

  • Bluetooth Low Energy: Low cost and long battery life for location based solutions
  • Raspberry Pi 3: Low cost and small form factor
  • SMS: Text message notification for user alerts
  • Websockets: Realtime update of events and statistics across all servers and clients
  • Node.js: Fast and secure server providing API access
Results

With the prototype's success, we received an offer for a venture partnership deal. The prototype was also chosen to be tested and piloted in private dementia clinics and facilities.

Kinect PhotoBooth

Government Research Product
Project

Our client was participating in a government research project on adopting motion sensing technology into consumer products. The client asked us to develop a novel photobooth product that leverages motion sensing technology

Approach

We applied several unique technical elements onto photobooths used in corporate events. The novel product we developed is currently patent pending.

We used motion sensor technology to make an autonomous photobooth with many user experience improvements. The photobooth is designed to recognize users's gestures in order to take photos, and collects useful traffic statistics.

A unique photo hosting and delivery system was designed to allow all members in group photos to retrieve and share their photos concurrently within seconds.

Technologies

The project featured solutions such as:

  • Motion sensing: The hardware solution we chose provided fast and highly accurate motion sensing for users' limbs, hands, head, and torso
  • Websockets: Photos taken at the booth were delivered at real time for users to keep
  • Node.js: A novel arrangement of devices and server logic allowed users to retrieve the photos in less than a second, and share directly to various social media channels
  • Client-side webapp: Administrators had real time access to system status and statistics
Results

The government officials from the research grant organization was excited about the product demonstration, and gave high praises. The phenomenal success of the research project evaluation lead to discussiosn for further projects.

A full utility patent for the product is currently being pursued by the client's legal team.

VR Safari

Government Research Product
Project

Our client was participating in a research project on creating consumer products using virtual reality. The client asked us to create and publish two virtual reality mobile apps on the Google Play Store.

Approach

The client provided us with their app designs and graphical assets. It was designed in consideration of current non-VR mobile games. As expected, we found that this led to severe performance issues when ported as a virtual reality application.

We used optimization techniques such as occlusion culling and light baking in order to dramatically increase the performance of the applications. We also identified parts of the application where a slight reduction of small animations or object detail can lead to significant performance boosts.

Although the initial project was for us to simply implement an application according to provided designs, we decided to make suggestions for UX changes in order to better fit a virtual reality application. These suggestions were not only well received, but also coupled with the clearance to make further modifications as we deem appropriate.

Technologies

The projecet featured solutions such as:

  • Google Cardboard: Users with standard mobile phones could experience virtual reality at low cost
  • Profiling: Multiple devices were profiled for their CPU/GPU/Memory usage for performance analysis
  • Optimization techniques: Techniques often used in game development, such as occlusion culling, were leveraged to create a high performance application
  • Google Play Store: The applications were distributed to users around the world via the Play Store
Results

After extensive profiling and optimizations, the framerate of the application was nearly doubled, dramatically reducing the chance of headaches and increasing immersion for the user.

With the added UX modifications, the mobile apps were solid products that could be released to the market.

This was our first project with this particular client, and its success convinced the client to trust us with designing and development of larger products.

Inventory Database Solution

Custom Business Product
Project

Our client posted a public request for developing a plugin for Microsoft Access that can generate PDF quotes using the data.

The client's company was about to start its computer database system, and had not picked a database solution.

Approach

We sent in a proposal for a complete system including the database, tag based search, and the PDF quote generation. The client was happy with the proposal, and we had the previlege of setting up the company's database system from ground up.

The system featured a SQL database accessible by Microsoft Access, and allowed the client to create tags for each items in order to easily search for them when building custom quotes. The PDF quote generation system automatically inserted price, image, dimensions, and a hyperlink to access more details.

Technologies

The system included features such as:

  • Simple interface that can be modified with HTML/CSS
  • Extensive item search system including text match, value range, and tags
  • PDF document generation according to client's design specifications
  • An "Open All" button in the PDF that opens multiple product listings with one click
  • Detailed installation, usage, and troubleshooting guide
Results

The client had a tight budget constraint, and we were able to create a bare-bones system with easy upgrade capabilities. The end product was able to met all of the client's needs as well as resource constraints.

With our knowledge of the client's needs, we continue to provide the client with updates and additional support, such as providing a method for automated backup.
